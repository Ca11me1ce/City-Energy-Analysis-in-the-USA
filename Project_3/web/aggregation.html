Aggregation

First, in order to distinguish the attributes of each category after merging, the corresponding category names are added to the variable names of the industrial, commercial and residential data sets: for example, in the industrial dataset, num_establishment was changed to i_num_establishment. Next, the three data sets are combined based on state and City to generate the file named energy_final.csv. Because the project was about energy usage situation, the urban energy classes of each city are extracted and combined them with cities in the same state. Based on the combined results, calculate the proportion of normal, high and very high in each class and generated the file class_state_percent.csv. Observing at the percentage of energy use levels in each state, to generate the energy use levels percentage for each state. The generation rules are as following: 1. If the high percentage is larger than 0.2, this state's level is high; If the very high percentage is larger than 0.1 and also larger than its high percentage, then this state's level is very high. 3. Otherwise, this state-level is normal.
