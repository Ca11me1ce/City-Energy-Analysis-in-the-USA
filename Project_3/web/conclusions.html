Aggregation

First, in order to distinguish the attributes of each category after merging, I added corresponding category names to the variable names of the industrial, commercial and residential data sets: for example, in industrial dataset, num_establishment was changed to i_num_establishment. Next, I combined the three data sets based on state and City to generate the file named energy_final.csv. Because the project was about energy usage situation, I extracted the urban energy classes of each city and combined them with cities in the same state. Based on the combined results, I calculated the proportion of normal, high and very high in each class and generated the file class_state_percent.csv. Observing at the percentage of energy use levels in each state, I've generated the energy use levels percentage for each state. The generation rules are as following: 1. If the high percentage is larger than 0.2, this state's level is high; If the very high percentage is larger than 0.1 and also larger than its high percentage, then this state's level is very high. 3. Otherwise, this state level is normal.

Conclusion and Discussion

With the scale of city, the level of the majority is normal energy consumption. The normal degree percentage is as high as 83.75%. The percentages of high and very high degrees are 10.79% and 5.46% individually. With the scale of the whole state, the level of the majority is still normal energy consumption. The normal degree percentage is as high as 80.53%. And the percentages of high and very high degrees are 12.81% and 6.65% respectively. While there are more cities with very high residential energy consumptions than high residential energy consumptions. However, the number of cities with high energy consumptions are more than those with very high energy consumptions in terms of commercial and industrial data. According to this analysis, the factors that have impacts on energy consumptions mainly include total population, total number of housing units, (commercial and industrial部分重要影响属性补充).

The government should pay more attention to the components with high and very high energy consumptions and proposes relevant policies such as disseminating the necessity of turning off lights when leaving, encouraging industries replace old equipment with new and power saving equipment.
