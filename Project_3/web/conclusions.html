Ethical Considerations

The source of these datasets is The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) which provides public APIs to collect data in the website. The data might be collected from the relevant statistics information such as population. All attributes in the datasets are anonymous and values of an area not any specific person. Besides, it is obvious that people know about the evaluations being conducted by using the information. This analysis could give people and organizations or companies who are interested in energy consumptions a detailed description about energy consumptions of each city in USA. Conclusion and Discussion

With the scale of the cities, the level of the majority is normal energy consumption. The normal degree percentage is as high as 83.75%. The percentages of high and very high degrees are 10.79% and 5.46% individually. With the scale of the whole state, the level of the majority is still normal energy consumption. The normal degree percentage is as high as 80.53%. And the percentages of high and very high degrees are 12.81% and 6.65% respectively. While there are more cities with very high residential energy consumptions than high residential energy consumptions. However, the number of cities with high energy consumption are more than those with very high energy consumptions in terms of commercial and industrial data. According to this analysis, the factors that have impacts on energy consumption mainly include total population, the total number of housing units. The number of buildings is very influenced by the commercial and industrial electricity and natural gas usage amount.

The government should pay more attention to the components with high and very high energy consumptions and proposes relevant policies such as disseminating the necessity of turning off lights when leaving, encouraging industries to replace old equipment with new and power-saving equipment.
