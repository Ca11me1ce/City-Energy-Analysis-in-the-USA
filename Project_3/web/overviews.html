Background

           With the development of human society, energy consumption and carbon dioxide emissions are increasing day by day, bringing great climate and environmental pressure, and threatening the survival of human society. As an area with intensive human activities, cities are the primary sources of energy consumption and carbon emissions, and their governance and development in energy conservation and emission reduction are increasingly valued by all countries. Energy consumption, monitoring, and management are crucial components of sustainable, eco-friendly infrastructures now and into the future.

           In the past, a building energy consumption analysis system is available which focuses on accurately determining building energy expenditures, performance, and costs. Besides, there is also energy consumption and cost-benefit analysis of hybrid and electric city buses. In 2017, real-time predictive systems for intelligent energy monitoring and management of electrical power networks are available. However, these systems are good tools for administrators to monitor and manage the electricity system to avoid any failure and interact with users. Instead of focusing on specific energy consumption, such as bus fuel, air-conditioning electricity consumption or management tools for owners, a need exists to analyze energy consumption on a larger scale, including most fields of our daily life and show the detailed analysis results or predicted energy consumption to the public. The object of this project is to provide intuitive senses about energy consumptions.

Data Science Problem

           Increasing environmental concerns and limited resources alter people that the significance of paying attention to saving energy. In order to have a better understanding of energy consumption and promote the development of energy conservation, this project is to obtain a detailed view of how energy consumption occurs in all fields of our daily life, such as residential, commercial and industrial fields with the analysis of energy data.

           This project analyzes and reviews the current situation of energy consumption in the United States in recent years and draws the conclusion of the factors that affect energy consumption and the relationship among the factors.

           This project also classifies the cities. It uses historical data to classify the cities in two-tier levels: the first tier is based on the type of energy use: industrial, commercial and residential; the second tier is based on energy use: extremely high electricity, high electricity, and normal electricity; extremely high gas consumption, high gas consumption, and normal gas consumption. In this way, we can add some labels to each city. For example, we can analyze that a city has extremely high electricity usage and high gas usage in industry, high electricity usage and high gas usage in commercial, and normal electricity usage and normal gas usage in residential.

           Moreover, this project can analyze the energy usage for each state by putting together the labels for all the cities in the same state, speculate the state current situation, and use the historical data training model to predict the future energy use of each city and each state. Finally, through the current status of the state and the prediction of the future, this project can give the state government some suggestions on energy use and future development.
