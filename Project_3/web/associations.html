Association Rules / Frequent Itemset Mining Analysis

Association rules mining is to find items that frequently occur together in the same transactions, it’s useful to find co-occurrence relationships in the dataset.

For the residential dataset, the subset contains 3 columns – ‘state_id’, ‘elec_degree’, ‘gas_degree’. Before calling the Apriori algorithm to calculate support, some transformation is necessary. Because the degree of electricity and gas are both 1 to 5. If using the raw subset to calculate, the algorithm would regard two attributes the same. Therefore, it is necessary to transform the two attributes into a string type. For example,

Use three support levels – 0.6, 0.1, 0.05. When support = 0.6 and confidence = 0.6, the output is:

There are only three rules. the degrees of energy consumptions (both electricity and natural gas) of most cities are very low level. Besides, cities that consumed little electricity also consume little natural gas.

When support = 0.1 and confidence = 0.6, the output is same with above:

When support = 0.05 and confidence = 0.6, the output is:

In addition to the above three rules, there are eight more rules. Most cities in Pennsylvania state consume normal-level energy resources. Many cities consume high-level and very high-level electricity. Many cities that consume normal-level natural gas might consume high-level electricity. And there are more cities in Pennsylvania, Illinois, and Texas states.

The most frequent pattern is ‘gas_degree_very_low’. This means that the natural gas consumptions of most cities are at a normal level. It is reasonable.

For the commercial dataset, a few attributes are selected to finish this analysis: state, elec_class, and gas_class. The number of states in this commercial dataset is 46, which is acceptable. Since elec_class and gas_class have the same label name: Normal, High, and Extremely High, elec_class or gas_class are added before the label to distinguish them. Now the data frame is like:

From the result, people can know that:

  1. Most states have normal gas usage.
  2. Most states have normal electricity usage, but the number of them is less than the number of normal gas usage states.
  3. Most states have both normal electricity usage and normal gas usage.
  4. Illinois(IL) state has normal electricity usage and normal gas usage.
  5. Texas(TX) state has normal gas usage.
