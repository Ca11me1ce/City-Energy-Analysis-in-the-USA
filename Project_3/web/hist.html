Histograms and Correlations

For residential dataset, the histograms contains 4 variables – ‘housing_units’, ‘total_pop’, ’ ‘elec_mwh’, ‘gas_mcf’. These variables all have a similar distribution and are all skewed right. The reason is that ‘housing_units’ and ‘total_pop’ are positively correlated with ‘elec_mwh’ and ‘gas_mcf’.

Here is the table of correlations between the 4 residential variables.

This table shows that ‘housing_units’ and ‘total_pop’ are both strong positive correlated to ‘elec_mwh’ and ‘gas_mcf’. This means the amount of housing and population is strong positive correlated to energy consumptions. Besides, the correlation between housing and population is also strongly positive. This is obvious. However, the correlation between ‘elec_mwh’ and ‘gas_mcf’ is not as strong as others. This is reasonable that a city whose electricity consumption is high level may not be a city whose natural gas consumption is also a high level.

Here is a set of scatterplot subplots of residential energy data. It also shows the same patter as the correlation matrix.

For the commercial dataset, 9 attributes are selected to plot the histograms: 'elec_score', 'gas_score', 'num_establishments', 'elec_1kdollars', 'elec_mwh', 'gas_1kdollars', 'gas_mcf', 'elec_lb_ghg', 'gas_lb_ghg'. The result is shown below:

From this result, all attributes’ histograms are left-skewed, which indicates that although the data already handled the maximum value before, there are still have many huge numbers in it. it is acceptable because some cities can have high-level energy usage and it is an exact problem this project wants to analyze and predict.

Three attributes are selected: elec_1ldollars, elec_mwh, and elec_lb_ghg. And I draw a set of scatter plots to show these attributes relationship.

From the result, these attributes are related to each other because their scatter plot is not randomly distributed. In the contrary，their points are dense into multiple straight lines of emission patterns. It may indicate that the three attributes may have multiple linear correlations.

For the industrial dataset, the histograms containing all features. These variables all have a similar distribution and are all skewed right except bin groups. The reason is that all other features are positively correlated with elec_mwh and elec_lb_ghg.

elec_lb_ghg, elec_mwh, and elec_1kdollars are identified for finding the correlation.

This table shows that elec_1kdollars is weakly correlated to elec_mwh and elec_1kdollars. but the correlation between elec_mwh and elec_lb_ghg is a strong positive. This is reasonable that the industry uses the most consumption of energy is either electricity or natural gas. And the set of scatter plot subplots is provided. It also shows the same pattern as the correlation matrix.
